Teaching Philosophy

I have 7+ years of experience tutoring across a wide range of subjects and student ages. I have always loved learning and helping people, so tutoring became a natural passion of mine. Over time, I have honed my ability to tailor lessons to each student's unique learning style and needs. Ultimately, my goal is to provide personalized tutoring that gives a complete understanding of the material. I believe that this way of learning, instead of rote memorization, makes the content enjoyable and applicable to future courses, tasks, or assessments.

If you're looking for a dedicated, experienced tutor, please reach out to schedule a lesson! Additionally, you can read some of my current and past students' reviews here. I look forward to working with you!

Education and Experience

I am an undergraduate at Yale University, where I study Applied Mathematics and French. I tutor for two calculus courses and serve as an academic coach for a summer program geared towards incoming freshmen who need extra preparation for college-level math. I have also continued to tutor privately, while managing a full courseload, holding leadership in clubs, participating in study abroad experiences, and gaining research experience in cognitive science, machine learning, and neural networks.

I graduated as the valedictorian of my high school and earned national recognized as a 2021 US Presidential Scholar. As the president of National Honor Society, I tutored middle school students and high school peers in math, science, and French, and I oversaw various learning initiatives in the community, such as book drives and tutoring programs. I also taught young children how to read through the AmeriCorps non-profit Reading Partners, volunteered as a tutor in local community centers, taught individual piano lessons, helped students prepare for the SAT/ACT, and reviewed college application essays (including for a Harvard-bound student).
